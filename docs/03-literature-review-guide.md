# Literature Review Guide

A literature review summarises, compares, and evaluates existing work on a topic. It should not be a simple list of papers. A strong literature review groups sources by theme, method, debate, or chronology and explains how the current project fits into the research landscape.

## Structure

Start with the scope of the review and the search strategy. Then organise the main body around themes or methodological approaches. End by identifying gaps, unresolved questions, and the contribution of the current project.

## Evaluation Criteria

When reviewing a source, consider its research question, dataset, method, findings, limitations, and relevance. Prefer comparing sources rather than summarising each paper in isolation.

## Common Mistakes

Common mistakes include over-quoting, failing to connect sources, ignoring limitations, and not explaining why the selected studies matter for the project.
