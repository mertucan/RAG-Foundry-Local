# Research Methodology Notes

Research methodology describes how a study is planned, conducted, and evaluated. A good methodology section explains the research question, data sources, sampling strategy, analysis method, limitations, and ethical considerations.

## Qualitative Methods

Qualitative research focuses on meanings, experiences, patterns, and interpretation. Common techniques include interviews, observations, thematic analysis, document analysis, and case studies. It is appropriate when the goal is to understand why or how something happens.

## Quantitative Methods

Quantitative research focuses on measurement, numerical data, variables, and statistical relationships. Common techniques include surveys, experiments, descriptive statistics, correlation, and regression. It is appropriate when the goal is to measure scale, frequency, or relationships between variables.

## Mixed Methods

Mixed methods combine qualitative and quantitative evidence. A project might use survey results to identify a trend, then interviews to explain why the trend appears.
